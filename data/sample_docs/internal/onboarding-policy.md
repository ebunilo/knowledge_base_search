# Employee Onboarding Policy (Internal)

**INTERNAL ONLY — DO NOT DISTRIBUTE**

This document describes the onboarding process for new employees at Acme.
It covers the first 30, 60, and 90 days, and lists the systems each role
needs access to.

## Day 0: Before the employee starts

The hiring manager must:

1. File a new-hire ticket in the HR portal at least 5 business days in advance.
2. Confirm the employee's department, role, and manager in Workday.
3. Request hardware from IT through the onboarding portal.

IT provisions the following before day 1:

- A laptop (MacBook Pro by default; Windows on request)
- A corporate email address of the form `firstname.lastname@acme.internal`
- SSO access to the baseline tool set

## Day 1 to Day 30: Orientation

On day 1, the new employee attends a company-wide orientation. HR covers
security training, benefits enrollment, and the employee handbook.

Managers should schedule weekly 1:1s for the first month and assign a
peer buddy.

## Day 30 to Day 60: Productivity ramp-up

The employee should:

- Complete their role-specific training curriculum.
- Deliver their first small project, reviewed by their manager.
- Provide feedback on the onboarding experience through the HR survey.

## Day 60 to Day 90: Full contribution

By the end of the 90-day probation:

- The employee is expected to operate independently on mid-complexity work.
- The manager files a probation-end evaluation in Workday.
- HR schedules the 90-day check-in.

| Product            | Jira, Confluence, Figma, Google Drive (product) |

| Finance            | NetSuite, Adaptive Planning, Coupa              |
| Legal              | iManage, DocuSign admin, Legal Vault S3         |

## PII and confidentiality

Every onboarding document references PII (home address, emergency contact,
bank details). HR must store these only in Workday. They must never be
emailed, Slacked, or stored in shared drives. Breaches must be reported to
privacy@acme.internal within 24 hours.

Contact HR: onboarding@acme.internal
